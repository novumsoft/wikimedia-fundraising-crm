Wikipedia is a global project. At the Wikimedia Foundation, we have engineers and other staff striving to make your experience on Wikipedia useful every day. We are one of the world’s most popular and beloved websites, and we are working to make Wikipedia accessible on any internet connection, across any device, no matter where you are in the world. We are standing up for the future of the open internet and fighting to protect the privacy of our users around the world. Each contribution matters, and your donation has a big impact.

Every day, people make critical decisions based on what they read on the internet. Wikipedia is a vital resource because it offers free, neutral, and unbiased information written by people from all around the world.

Wikipedia would not be possible without the shared commitment from our readers, editors, and donors like you. We’re able to stay neutral and unbiased because you support our independence. People know they can rely on Wikipedia because we are not influenced by any outside stakeholders. We are here to make knowledge available to all. This trust is the heart of our mission: we are here for you and you are here for us.

By donating, you are safeguarding the sum of human knowledge now and for generations to come.

We’re engaged in big picture planning for the future of Wikipedia and the role we want to play in the world. Your support makes us optimistic as we forge onwards. We can’t thank you enough for supporting Wikipedia, and we hope you will continue to stand on the side of knowledge. If you have friends who use Wikipedia, please invite them to donate on Facebook or Twitter.

With gratitude,
Katherine Maher,
Executive Director, Wikimedia Foundation
